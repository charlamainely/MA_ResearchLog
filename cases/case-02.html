Case Study

Investigating dsvh.gov.vn by Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism

Creator/Studio: Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism Year: Unknown Context: Digital archive Why selected: Examining how a govermental agency is digitizing cultural heritage

Overview

Website claims to be a repository for digitized cultural heritage. It appears legitimate, but is not promoted anywhere, I stumbled upon it during random google searches. But is said to be supported by an official government agency - Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism.

Images

Key Takeaways

  • Website is well-designed and provides categorization of documented heritage.
  • Has a separate page for intangible cultural heritage (though no further classification provided).
  • Quite prolific documentation of cultural heritage in all categories. Also includes news and official announcements regarding
  • cultural heritage, so it’s updated and provides more recent information as well.
  • Website seems to be update frequently.
  • However, website is still quite rudimentary as items are documented using purely text, no images are given. Text only in Vietnamese, so non-Vietnamese speakers would struggle to find value in this.

Reflection

Overall, a great effort to document and classify cultural heritage using a digital platform by thr Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism. However, it’s still only archival and provides one way information, and doesn’t encourage any engagement activity. Seeing that only about 1 million people has accessed the page since launched shows engagement is quite low, and I wouldn’t know what to do on this page anyway.
