Case Study

Investigating disanso.vn by Human Interaction & Robotics Lab of National University of Hanoi

Creator/Studio: Human Interaction & Robotics Lab - School of Information Technology, National University of Hanoi Year: Unknown Context: Digital archive Why selected: Examining projects that are digitizing cultural heritage using emerging technology

Overview

A project by the Human interaction and robotics lab, school of information technology, Hanoi National University. This archive aims to document Vietnamese cultural heritage using a variety of media - 3D models, 3D animation, documentaries, recordings,…

Images

Key Takeaways

  • The website isn’t optimized for viewing or designed purposefully, and is quite hard to find or access the website in the first place.
  • There’s no classification system for the archive, and search function is quite useless since we don’t really know what to look for.
  • There menu is extremely confusing, and doesn't directly lead to the archive. Items are documented as one big list, and has to rely on search function to find anything, menu doesn't help with anything, links all go to other unrelated pages. Items don't have useful thumbnail so I don't know what each item is.
  • Each item has further text information to explain more, it's just long continuous paragraphs, no structure or headings to support text digestion. Based on the view count of items documented, engagement is quite low and it’s unclear the goal of the archive (not stated anywhere).

Reflection

The archive creates a valuable source of information, however, the website lacks appeal and purpose. But it’s interesting to see the introduction of emerging technologies for the purpose of preservation, specifically photogrammetry and reconstruction of ICH using 3D animation.

This is an example technology adoption for cultural preservation, and perhaps integration of interactivity, either for introduction of the page or to navigate/highlight important artifacts can help users experience on this platform.
