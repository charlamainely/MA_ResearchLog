Week 05 — Storytelling as ICH Practice & Multilayer Prototype Idea

Dates: 2025-09-15 → 2025-09-21

Activities

  • Reflected on the topic and expanded focus from preserving specific folktales to preserving the living practice of storytelling in Vietnamese culture. This reframes ICH in a more future-forward way and suggests a pathway for communities—especially minority groups—to preserve their own culture.
  • Sketched a lo-fi prototype idea for multilayer storytelling as an interactive design, inspired by fieldwork at Singapore Design Week over the weekend.

Reflection

When I presented my ideas to Joselyn last week, she asked how I envision the final design—what kind of game it would be. I described the concept I had then: a branching narrative game based on Vietnamese folktales where players make storytelling choices that affect variations of the story (not the outcome), showing how communities generate their own versions.

After thinking it through, that idea felt restrictive: the stories themselves would mostly stay the same, and the nuanced variations might not be emphasized enough. That led me to storytelling as a practice that can be preserved. In this reframing, the game becomes a facilitator for communities to practice storytelling and share stories.

Looking at oral traditions in Vietnam, a common thread is that messages are encoded in stories—transmitted through different media. Fieldwork at Singapore Design Week reinforced that generational wisdom and culture are often passed on as stories, which are easy to spread and document.

If we strengthen the practice of storytelling, we can empower communities with a method to preserve their intangible cultural heritage and provide a platform to share stories more effectively.

The challenge is scope: “storytelling” is broad. To make it digestible, I’ll investigate a specific genre of Vietnamese stories and extract both the storytelling structure and the encoded themes. Using that structure and theme set, I can create a prototype sandbox game where participants learn about structure and craft their own stories with relevant themes.
