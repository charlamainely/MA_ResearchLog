Week 8 - Formative Feedback and Making the Archive More Playful

Dates: 2026-07-06 → 2026-07-12

Activities

  • Present table display and exegesis progress for formative feedback
  • Identify text-heavy areas in the display and plan visual simplification
  • Redesign story submission page into a more playful portal

Reflection

During formative feedback this week, I received the comment that my table display still has some areas that are too heavy in research text content. This is fair, and honestly something I could already sense while building it, but hearing it again confirmed that I still need to simplify the information and better match it to the playful design direction of my prototype display. The overall content is there, but now the challenge is to make the key ideas visually clear rather than letting them get buried in too much text.

I was also advised to focus more strongly on showing the user testing feedback and outcomes in my exegesis, and more importantly, to clearly indicate where the change happened because of that feedback. I think this is a very useful note, because the most valuable part of this project is not only the prototypes themselves, but the fact that they changed through real family testing. So the writing needs to better highlight that iterative process and show how user testing shaped the research direction and design decisions over time.

For the website, I made a more playful update to the story submission flow. Previously, the story submission page looked more like a standard form, which worked functionally but did not really feel aligned with the tone of the project. So I redesigned it into something closer to a game-like portal, where the user goes through a more playful sequence with surprise prompts and postcard decoration stages before submitting their story. This feels much more fitting with the rest of the prototypes, since the archive now does not just store stories, but also extends the same playful interaction style into the digital space.

So overall, this week was less about making entirely new things, and more about making the existing materials clearer, more cohesive, and more aligned with the overall tone of the project. I think this kind of feedback stage is really important, because it helps reveal where the research is already strong, and where the presentation still needs to catch up with the quality of the work itself.
