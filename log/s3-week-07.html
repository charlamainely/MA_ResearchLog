Week 7 - Mockups, Modular Fixes, and Writing Properly

Dates: 2026-06-29 → 2026-07-05

Activities

  • Print and test mockup of table display on the physical table
  • Continue writing exegesis section by section
  • Refine story archive website and connect it to a working backend database

Reflection

This week I printed out a mockup of my table display and mapped it onto the actual table. This was very useful, because while the digital layout helped me think through the overall structure, seeing the content on the real table immediately made it obvious where the text was too small, where the information distribution felt too dense, and which parts still needed more breathing room.

After seeing the mockup in place, I made notes on text scale and content distribution, and then kept making minor adjustments to the table throughout the week. What was quite nice is that I could update the display in a very modular way by printing revised sections and just pasting them over the existing mockup. This turned out to be quite an easy and efficient method for iterating the display physically without having to reprint the entire thing every time something changes.

I also started focusing more seriously on the exegesis. After fleshing out the main sections and subsections last week, I began slowly filling them in section by section each day. The useful thing is that across the 3 semesters, a lot of the material has actually already been written somewhere, just scattered across different formats. Some of it is in my research logs, some in conference submissions, some in presentations, scripts, exhibition display text, and previous documentation. So for earlier chapters such as the introduction and contextual development, the task is less about inventing new writing and more about stitching these texts together into one coherent structure.

The same goes for the prototype development chapter, since many of those developments are already documented in my logs and process pages. What feels newest and most difficult to write are the user testing and evaluation sections, as well as the conclusion, since these require me to step back and interpret what everything now means rather than just documenting what I did.

This week I also started refining the story archive more seriously. Previously it was mainly an MVP front-end mockup, but now I redesigned the website to match the overall art direction of my project, and linked the front-end pages to a backend database that can actually receive and store story entries. So the site can now function not only as a story viewing portal, but also as a submission portal, which feels like a major step toward making it useful beyond just being a visual mockup.
