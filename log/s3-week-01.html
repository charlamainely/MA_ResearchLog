Week 1 - Repacking the Prototypes

Dates: 2026-05-18 → 2026-05-24

Activities

  • Redesign board game based on Summative Feedback and Semester 2 user testing
  • Reframe prototypes as one boxed product played in sequence
  • Reach out to families in Vietnam for upcoming playtesting

Reflection

After the Summative Feedback from last semester, I started redesigning my board game again, which is part of Design Brief 1 where I create a collection of playful prototypes to engage families in deeper conversations. One of the feedback points that I really liked is the suggestion that the prototypes could be played in a sequence rather than as separate disconnected objects. So, instead of presenting them as individual pieces, I started packaging them as one overall product: a box that contains the activity book, the conversation deck, and the intergenerational board game.

At the same time, I knew the board game itself needed another round of redesign based on the user testing data from last semester. The biggest concern was that the mechanics were still too heavy, especially for older players, and I wanted to simplify the system so that it is easier to understand and easier to enter into without feeling intimidated. I also wanted to introduce more storytelling mechanics into the game, because although the first version had interesting generational differences, it still needed stronger prompts for players to actually talk to each other during play.

Outside of making, I also started reaching out to families in Vietnam who might be interested in being my playtesters, as I knew I would need to organise my trip back soon. This part already felt a bit daunting because not only do I have to explain my project clearly, but I also have to convince multiple members of a family to gather together and participate. Still, it felt good to finally begin arranging the real testing phase, as it made the whole project feel much more concrete.
