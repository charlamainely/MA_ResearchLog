Weekly Log

Week 5 - Three Tests and a Suitcase Full of Prints

Dates: 2026-06-22 → 2026-06-28

Activities

  • Produce 3D-printed game pieces and spinner for the game kit
  • Revise conversation deck and conduct second and third user testing sessions
  • Print and package final game kit elements before flying back to Singapore

Reflection

This week I worked on some of the more physical elements of the game kit. I modelled player characters in Blender to be used as game pawns and had them 3D printed, and I also had the spinner 3D printed to be added to the game kit. This part was definitely fun, and it made the board game feel much more like a real product rather than just a prototype made of flat printed parts.

I also added an additional 24 cards to the conversation deck, because the earlier 24-card version seemed to run out too quickly during play, and players also tended to replace the cards often when they were not interested in the topic. Another issue that came up is that younger players sometimes felt left out, because some questions relied heavily on reminiscing and comparing experiences that they simply do not have yet because they are too young. So I revised some of the questions to make them more inclusive, and by the end of this process I had a fuller deck of 48 cards.

I had my second user testing session this week, and the results showed that the storytelling mechanic I added to the board game was still not very engaging, and it was still a bit challenging for older generations to understand the rules and mechanics. So although the game was improving, it was clear that more work was still needed for all generations to meaningfully interact with each other throughout the game, rather than just play beside each other.

I also had my third and final user testing session, this time with all three generations in one family. However, because tension arose during the session, I only managed to play the card deck and not the board game. Even though this was not what I had planned, it still gave me a lot of useful insights and plenty of stories. In some ways, the tension itself is an important finding, because it shows how difficult it really is to create the conditions for intergenerational conversation, even when the activity is framed playfully.

After that, I printed all the elements of the game kit and packaged a few copies to give out to my playtesters as thank-you gifts. Then I flew back to Singapore with all my prints, materials, test data, and footage from the user testing, ready to start synthesising everything. It was honestly a very packed and slightly chaotic week, but I feel like I ended it with a much clearer sense of what is working and what still needs to be changed.
