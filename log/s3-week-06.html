Week 6 - Turning Testing into a Display

Dates: 2026-06-22 → 2026-06-28

Activities

  • Synthesize user testing data for consultation
  • Start designing WIP table display layout and visitor journey
  • Draft the outline of exegesis

Reflection

After coming back from Vietnam, I mainly focused on synthesizing all the user testing data I collected so that I could present it clearly during this week’s consultation. Since I came back with a lot of notes, footage, stories, and observations across multiple families, this step was really about condensing the material into something I could actually reflect on and discuss. It also helped me clarify what parts of the prototypes were working, what parts were still too difficult, and what kinds of patterns were starting to appear across the sessions.

After that, I started designing the layout of my display tables and thinking about how I can translate all the information from my research into a visual exhibition format. This turned out to be much harder than I expected. The table space is not actually small, but compared to the amount of information I have accumulated over 3 semesters, it suddenly felt far too limited. So the main challenge is not just how to fill the space, but how to filter the information and decide what is actually relevant enough to show, and then how to present it in a way that still feels cohesive and aesthetically aligned with the prototypes.

So I first mapped out the table digitally, using my design framework as a guide. I tried to think of the display as a kind of viewer journey, similar to how I think through the player journey in my prototypes. Dividing the table into 3 main areas, I have one section that shows the earlier exploration phase and underlying research foundation, before moving into information about the prototypes and their development stages, and finally a summary of the user testing and feedback synthesis. This helped me feel less overwhelmed, because at least now I have a clear structure to work within rather than just placing things around randomly.

I also started drafting the outline of my exegesis this week. At this stage it is mostly about structuring the document and identifying the main sections, rather than writing in full. But having spent so much time making, testing, and documenting already, it felt important to finally begin turning all of that into a long-form written argument.
